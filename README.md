📊 Key Insights

📌 Out of 7,367 total students, 6,801 attended exams, indicating a high overall participation rate (~92%).

📌 5,225 students successfully passed, while 1,576 students have academic backlog, highlighting a notable performance gap.

📌 Engineering faculty has the highest number of students and also the highest number of unexcused absences (356), making it a key area for attendance improvement.

📌 IT faculty demonstrates the strongest performance balance, with high attendance and a relatively lower absence rate compared to its size.

📌 Management faculty, despite having the smallest number of students, shows a comparatively better academic performance ratio.

📌 Full-time students dominate exam participation, while part-time student engagement is significantly lower, suggesting a disparity in involvement.

📌 Course-level analysis shows that early-year students (I–III) have higher participation rates, while participation decreases in later courses.

📌 Performance indicators across faculties reveal that although Engineering has scale, its quality indicator is lower compared to Management, indicating potential efficiency issues.

📌 A large proportion of students fall into the “A,B,C,D,E” grade category, suggesting moderate overall performance, with relatively few top-performing (A-only) students.

📌 Unexcused absences (566 total) represent a critical issue and directly impact overall academic success rates.
